Please review the internship position descriptions  for details on responsibilities and commitments required of the position. We are looking for enthusiastic, reliable, mindful, hard working individuals who are passionate about growing local food, sustainable (regenerative) agriculture and who would make a positive contribution to our farm teams. 

Interns are expected to take their role very seriously and must be able to commit to one work day per week from March through to October.  Once the CSA season begins in July, interns will commit to an additional half day of harvesting for the CSA.  Interns are also required to attend an orientation session on March 8th and 9th 2013 from 4-6pm (with optional dinner to follow).

Interns receive ongoing support during their placement as well as two farm T-shirts, a weekly intern harvest box, and a letter of reference upon completion of the program.  

If you are interested in interning with us please read the position description thoroughly and fill out the following application form carefully. Applications will be considered on a first-come-first-served basis and the first selection will be based entirely on the information you provide on your form. The final deadline for application is February 10th.   If you are not called by March 18th, it is unlikely that you have been selected this year. We do encourage you to try again next year. 

We are looking forward to hearing from you!

Emi Do
